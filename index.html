🍜 Food

Toronto is an excellent vegan city. These are all either fully vegan or very vegan-friendly. The Annex and Kensington are particularly well-covered.

Breakfast & coffee

Moonbean Coffee
Long-running Kensington Market café — atmospheric, lived-in, vegan-friendly food. Perfect morning stop before the market opens up around you. Cash-friendly.
📍 30 St. Andrew St, Kensington · maps
Almond Butterfly Bakeshop
Gluten-free café + bakery one block south of Bloor. Daily 9am–7pm. Pastries, coffee, dairy-free options. Good first-morning spot given how close it is to the Airbnb.
📍 100 Harbord St · maps
Jimmy's Coffee
Beloved Toronto indie coffee with multiple locations. The College St spot is the closest — unpretentious, good espresso, easy to duck into between sets or before the day starts.
📍 395 College St · maps
Fresh Kitchen + Juice Bar
Fully vegan, on Bloor — good for a late morning or early lunch between days. Cauliflower wings, poutine, bowls. Daily from 11am.
📍 386 Bloor St W · maps

Groceries

📅 Sunday May 31 — Pedestrian Sunday

Kensington Market goes car-free noon–8pm on the last Sunday of every month. Street performers, pop-up vendors, the whole neighborhood transforms. You'll be right there for it — plan to wander Kensington in the afternoon before the Sunday night shows.

Fiesta Farms
Toronto's best-loved independent grocery — large, well-stocked, great bulk section and produce. About 10 min walk from the Airbnb. Good for stocking up on snacks and breakfast items for the weekend.
📍 200 Christie St · maps
Kensington Market
Not one shop — the whole neighborhood. Produce stands, cheese shops, bulk food, bakeries, butchers with good veg options. May 31 is a Sunday — last Sunday of the month means Pedestrian Sunday, the market goes car-free with vendors and street performers.
📍 Augusta Ave / Baldwin St · maps
Big Carrot
Worker-owned organic/natural food co-op, very veg-forward. Worth knowing about if you end up on the east side of the city — it's a trek from the Annex but a good food-nerd destination.
📍 348 Danforth Ave · maps

Late night

Sneaky Dee's
Legendary Toronto punk bar — nachos served until 3am. The right call after a 1am closer. College & Bathurst, about 10 min walk from Lee's. Tex-Mex, cheap drinks, has been part of the local punk/indie scene for decades. Downstairs bar is the move.
📍 431 College St · maps

Records

Rotate This!
The Toronto record store for this crowd — punk, metal, experimental, indie. New and used. Has been the city's underground music hub for decades. Don't skip it.
📍 186 Ossington Ave · maps
She Said Boom!
Used vinyl and books — strong selection for rock, punk, experimental, and jazz. Two floors, very browsable. Good if you want to dig without a specific target.
📍 372 College St · maps
Kops Records
One of Toronto's largest used vinyl selections — massive, chaotic in a good way. Worth the trip out to Danforth if you want to really dig. Better for classic rock and metal than the others.
📍 1984 Danforth Ave · maps

🖼 Art & Culture

Art Gallery of Ontario
Big collection worth wandering — Frank Gehry redesign. Easy to do in a few hours between festival days.
📍 317 Dundas St W · maps
The Power Plant
Industrial waterfront gallery, contemporary/avant-garde shows. Usually free and compact.
📍 Harbourfront · maps
Distillery District + Arta Gallery
Pedestrian historic district with galleries, brick alleys, and patios. Good afternoon wander.
📍 Distillery District · maps
Royal Ontario Museum
World-class collection — medieval, Egyptian, ancient Near East, Celtic artifacts, natural history. The Libeskind crystal wing addition is striking architecture. Five-minute walk from Trinity St. Paul's, so a Friday or Saturday morning before 5pm is the obvious window. ~$25 CAD. The most relevant art institution on this list given the interests in the group.
📍 100 Queens Park · maps
Graffiti Alley
Rush Lane — the alley behind Queen St W between Spadina and Portland. Continuous mural installation stretching several blocks, constantly evolving. Free, takes 20 minutes, genuinely impressive. Easy to walk on the way to or from Kensington or Rotate This.
📍 Rush Lane, Queen St W · maps

⚡ Practical Notes

  • 👟 Walking festival — all venues are in the Annex or nearby. Comfortable shoes matter more than anything else you pack.
  • 📱 Download the DICE app before you go — your tickets live there. Don't assume you can pull them up in a browser at the door.
  • 🔞 Most venues are 19+. Trinity St. Paul's and Tranzac are all-ages. Bring ID regardless.
  • 🗺 Lee's Palace and Trinity St. Paul's are both on Bloor St W, about a 3-minute walk apart. Tranzac is one block south on Brunswick. Plan your venue hops accordingly.
  • 🍺 Tranzac has its own bar and a more relaxed vibe — good spot to decompress between heavy sets.
  • 📅 Trail of Dead (performing Source Tags & Codes + Secret of Elena's Tomb in full) is listed as Friday but didn't appear in the timetable image at time of writing — double check the schedule on the PTG site before Friday.
